The problem with data breaches in India

India has become one of the most breached countries in the world. Every year, tens of millions of Indian users have their personal data exposed through breaches at platforms they trust daily — food delivery apps, grocery services, payment wallets, job portals, airlines. The data ends up on dark web forums, gets sold in bulk, and eventually gets used for phishing, identity fraud, and credential stuffing attacks.

The frustrating part is that users are almost never told directly. Companies issue vague press statements, regulators rarely enforce notification obligations, and by the time news reaches the public, the damage is already done. Hackers don't wait for press releases.

Most people only discover their data was compromised after something bad has already happened — a fraudulent transaction, a hacked Instagram account, an OTP scam. We wanted to give people a way to check before that happens.

What Shadow Profile actually does

When you type your email into Shadow Profile, we send a query to the HaveIBeenPwned database — one of the most comprehensive and trusted breach intelligence services in the world, maintained by security researcher Troy Hunt. It aggregates billions of records from thousands of documented data breaches and lets you check whether your email appeared in any of them.

We do not store your email. We do not log your query. The request goes out, the result comes back, and that's it — nothing is written to any database on our end. Your scan is ephemeral by design.

How the scan works — step by step

You enter your email → we send it to the HIBP API over an encrypted connection → HIBP returns a list of breach names and affected data types → we compute your Shadow Score based on severity, recency, and data sensitivity → results are displayed directly in your browser → nothing is stored anywhere.

The Shadow Score is our own threat-assessment layer on top of the raw breach data. A score of 0–25 means limited exposure. 26–50 means moderate risk from older or less critical breaches. 51–75 is high risk — passwords or phone numbers are in circulation. Above 75 is critical — multiple high-severity breaches with sensitive data actively traded.

What happens to your data after a breach

Most people assume a data breach is just an embarrassing moment for a company — a news story that blows over. The reality is more persistent. Once your data is in the wild, it tends to stay there and compound over time.

Credential stuffing is one of the most common downstream effects. Attackers take leaked email-password combinations and systematically try them across hundreds of platforms. If you use the same password in multiple places — and most people do — a single breach can cascade into five or six compromised accounts.

Targeted phishing becomes dramatically easier with breach data. An attacker who knows your name, employer, approximate location, and delivery address can craft messages that are almost indistinguishable from legitimate ones. The Domino's GPS data, for example, makes it trivial to write a message referencing your "recent order" to a specific address.

SIM swapping and OTP fraud — endemic in India — are frequently enabled by breached phone numbers combined with social engineering of telecom support staff.

The good news: most of these attacks are opportunistic, not targeted. Taking action within a reasonable window — changing passwords, enabling 2FA, locking your Aadhaar biometrics — significantly reduces your exposure even after a breach has occurred.

Common questions

Is my email address safe when I use this? ▶
Yes. We never store, log, or share your email. It's used only to query the breach database in real time and discarded immediately. There's no account creation, no sign-in, no cookie tracking your query history.
Where does the breach data come from? ▶
Email breach data is sourced from the HaveIBeenPwned API — a globally trusted service that aggregates billions of records from thousands of documented breaches. Phone number checks use locally processed datasets built from publicly available Indian breach data. We do not scrape or buy data from any third party.
My scan came back clean. Am I definitely safe? ▶
A clean result means your email hasn't appeared in any breach we have on record — which covers thousands of publicly documented incidents. However, many breaches are never disclosed, take years to surface, or haven't been indexed yet. A clean scan is a good sign but not a guarantee. Basic hygiene — unique passwords, 2FA enabled — is always worthwhile regardless of your score.
Why does the full report cost money? ▶
The HIBP API that powers the email scan has a usage cost. Rather than monetise your data or show advertising, we charge a small one-time fee — ₹49 or ₹99 — for the detailed report. No subscription. No recurring charge. The free scan always remains free.
What should I do if my email was breached? ▶
First — don't panic. Then: change your password on the affected platform and anywhere you reused it. Enable two-factor authentication. Check active sessions on major accounts and revoke unfamiliar ones. If financial data was involved, notify your bank. Our paid report includes a step-by-step checklist tailored to the specific breaches in your scan.
Can I check a phone number? ▶
Yes — phone number checking is available in the ₹99 Full Shadow Report. No OTP required. No number stored. We hash your number locally and check it against our database of known Indian breach records. The result tells you which known breach databases your number appeared in.
